About

I am a Civil and Environmental Engineer with a strong passion for Earth Observation (EO) and data science applied to environmental and climate challenges. I hold a Master’s degree from the University of Brescia (Italy) with a final grade of 110/110, where I conducted a thesis research focused on analyzing satellite-derived lake surface water temperature data to study heatwave patterns. Since then, I have expanded my expertise through a research fellowship, contributing to a future scientific publication on heat extremes in lakes, and through my current role as Data Scientist at MEEO, an ESA industrial partner. Here, I work with a variety of satellite datasets and contribute to the development of AI/ML solutions, including convolutional neural networks for super-resolution of Sentinel-2 imagery.

My main interests lie at the intersection of data science and climate science, with the aim of developing innovative solutions to monitor, understand, and address environmental changes.

Work

I have worked in both academic and industry settings, contributing to research, innovation, and outreach. From developing methodologies for satellite data analysis to managing large-scale science communication projects, my experience bridges technical expertise and effective dissemination of complex concepts.
